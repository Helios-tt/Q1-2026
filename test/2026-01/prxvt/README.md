## Root cause analysis

- **Title**: Transferable stPRXVT receipt tokens let fresh addresses replay historical staking rewards
- **Severity**: `critical`
- **Confidence**: `high`
- **Violated invariant**: A transferred staking receipt balance must not carry entitlement to rewards accrued before the recipient owned that balance unless the corresponding reward debt is transferred or settled.

### Final root cause

PRXVTStaking calculates rewards from the current stPRXVT balance and the caller's own userRewardPerTokenPaid, but stPRXVT transfers do not settle or transfer reward debt. A fresh helper contract can receive stPRXVT with userRewardPerTokenPaid equal to zero, call claimReward(), and be credited for historical rewardPerToken accrual that happened before it held the stake. claimReward() then burns the fee and transfers PRXVT from the staking pool, causing direct asset loss.

### Affected contracts

| Address | Name | Role | Implementation |
|---|---|---|---|
| `0xdac30a5e2612206e2756836ed6764ec5817e6fff` | `PRXVTStaking` | `primary vulnerable contract` | `—` |

### Recommended fixes

- Override PRXVTStaking's ERC20 _update/transfer path to settle rewards for both sender and receiver before balance movement and initialize the receiver's userRewardPerTokenPaid to the current rewardPerTokenStored for the transferred amount, or make stPRXVT non-transferable except mint and burn.
- Add invariant tests that transferring stPRXVT to a new address cannot increase total claimable PRXVT across sender plus receiver.
